Model Design Memo

Bharat-40B — an India-First 40B LLM

Data, cleaning, evaluation and tokenizer plan for a 40B dense model at Gemma-4-class quality — best-in-class at coding & agentic work, fluent across all 22 scheduled Indian languages, and India-first by default in context, units, law and worldview.

~10T pre-train tokens RLVR + India-first Constitutional AI Fertility = first-class KPI Vocab 256,000 → padded 262,144
Design premise. Indic text is <3% of Common Crawl and much of it lives in books, PDFs, legacy-font web pages and audio — not clean digital text. So the hard problems are sourcing and cleaning Indic data, and tokenizing ~11 scripts efficiently. Every decision below is optimized around that.

1.Design goals → what "India-first" actually means

"India-first" is a product requirement, not a slogan. The model defaults to Indian framing unless asked otherwise — the same way US models default to a US frame:

  • Units & format: INR (₹), km, DD/MM/YYYY, Indian numbering (lakh/crore), IST.
  • Legal & civic frame: Constitution of India, IPC/BNS, RBI/SEBI regulation as the default reference world.
  • Geopolitical stance: Jammu & Kashmir and Arunachal Pradesh treated as Indian territory; maps and answers consistent with the Indian position.
  • Language reality: excellent at code-mixed Hinglish / Tanglish and all 22 scheduled languages — not just Hindi — because that is how Indians actually type.

3.Pre-training data mix (~10T tokens)

Chinchilla-optimal for 40B is ~0.8–1T; we deliberately over-train (~10T, à la Gemma 3 27B @ 14T[3]) for inference-time strength.

India-specific sourcing moves that others skip

  • OCR-at-scale pipeline over digitized Indian books, newspapers & Archive.org regional collections — where most Indic text actually is.
  • Legacy-font recovery: millions of Indic pages use non-Unicode fonts (Kruti Dev, Shree-Lipi). Detect + convert to Unicode instead of discarding.
  • Synthetic Indic: translate high-quality English code/reasoning/textbooks into T1/T2 languages (quality-filtered) to close the volume gap without diluting quality.

Indic data is sourced primarily from AI4Bharat/Sangraha, IndicCorp, per-language Wikipedia, government open data (data.gov.in), Bhashini corpora, and licensed regional-news partnerships — not just Common Crawl, whose Indic coverage is thin and noisy.

4.Post-training (SFT) data

CodingRepo-level instruction data (multi-file edits, PR description → diff), competitive programming with unit tests, code-review/critique pairs, fill-in-the-middle. Prompts also authored in Hindi/Indic.
AgenticMulti-turn tool-calling transcripts (search, code exec, calculator, mock IRCTC/UPI-style Indian APIs), long-horizon task decomposition, and error-recovery traces where the agent fails and retries.
IndicNative instruction–response pairs written by in-language annotators (not MT'd from English, which imports Western framing), plus translation/summarization across all 22 languages and Hinglish chat.
India-firstQA over the Constitution, Indian history/geography/civics (NCERT/UPSC-aligned), exam prep (JEE/NEET/CAT), and explicit "default framing" examples (₹, km, DD/MM/YYYY, correct maps).

5.RL / alignment

  • RLVR (verifiable rewards) for code and math — the biggest reasoning lever: unit-test pass/fail and symbolic/numeric answer checking, matching Sarvam-M's reported math/programming gains via RLVR[1].
  • Execution-feedback / agentic RL: reward shaping over full tool-use trajectories (task success, step efficiency, safe-abort on ambiguous or destructive actions) — not just final-answer reward.
  • RLHF/RLAIF with Indian annotator pools across at least the top 9 languages, so preferences reflect Indian register, honorifics and cultural sensitivity — not a US/EU crowd.
  • India-first Constitutional AI grounded in constitutional values (pluralism, secularism, linguistic equality); handles caste/religion/region sensitivity with Indian legal context (IT Rules, BNS).
  • Safety / red-teaming in Indic + Hinglish — most safety filters leak in code-mixed text.

6.Cleaning pipeline

  1. Language & script ID: generic tools (cld3/langid) misclassify Indic heavily; use IndicLID/fastText-Indic per script, and explicitly tag romanized Indic rather than mislabel it as English.
  2. Normalization: Unicode NFC; canonicalize ZWJ/ZWNJ in Devanagari/Perso-Arabic; unify digit systems with a reversible tag; repair mojibake + legacy fonts.
  3. Deduplication: MinHash/LSH near-dedup globally, a second per-language pass (English thresholds under-clean low-resource boilerplate), plus cross-lingual dedup to drop near-duplicate translations.
  4. Quality filtering: where good per-language classifiers don't exist, use in-language perplexity filters + heuristics (line length, symbol ratio, nav-menu/boilerplate) rather than reusing an English classifier.
  5. Code: permissive-license only; exact + near dedup (code is extremely duplicated); execution-based filtering — keep snippets whose extracted tests pass; strip secrets/PII; drop minified/auto-generated.
  6. Math: symbolic/numeric answer verification to drop corrupted pairs; keep step-by-step derivations, filter answer-only noise; keep well-formed LaTeX.
  7. Safety/PII: scrub India-specific PII (Aadhaar, PAN, phone regexes); toxicity/CSAM filtering with classifiers validated on Indic scripts (English-trained ones have high false-negatives on Hindi/Bengali/Tamil).
  8. Decontamination: n-gram overlap removal against every eval benchmark below, run per language.

Why India-specific evals matter beyond generic ones: on SimpleQA-IN (10 Indic languages) a knowledge-augmented Sarvam setup scored 59% vs 11–47% for o3-mini/o3[1], and translation-tuned Sarvam-1 hit chrF++ 39.83 on En→Indic Flores vs 34.23 for Llama-3.1-8B[2] — gaps generic English-centric benchmarks would never surface.

8.Fertility targets & tokenizer sizing (the quantitative core)

Fertility = tokens per word (lower = cheaper, faster, more real content per context window). The failure mode to avoid is the Llama/GPT pattern where Hindi is 3–5× and Tamil/Telugu 5–8× English cost — Dravidian scripts are worst (agglutinative, syllabic akshara clusters). Reference points: Gemma 2/3 use a ~256k SentencePiece vocab[3][4]; Sarvam's 68k Indic-tuned vocab reaches ~1.4–2.1 tok/word on Indic vs 4–8 for generic tokenizers[5]; IndicSuperTokenizer shows a ~200k vocab covering English + 22 Indic + code hits SOTA fertility, and fertility stops improving materially past ~200k[6].

Targets

Language / domain groupTarget fertilityRationale

Tier-1 Indic (Hi, Bn, Mr, Te, Ta, Gu, Kn, Ml, Pa, Or)1.4–1.6 tok/wordMatches/beats Sarvam's range[5]; largest dedicated vocab share
Tier-2 scheduled (As, Ur, Sa, Kok, Sd, Ks, Mai, Bodo, Sat…)1.8–2.2 tok/wordLower-resource; accept some inefficiency vs starving Tier-1
Romanized / code-mixed Indic (Hinglish)~1.5 tok/wordReuses Latin merges + high-frequency romanized Indic morphemes
Code (Py, JS/TS, Java, C/C++, Go, Rust, SQL)~0.30–0.35 tok/charStarCoder/Gemma-class; whitespace/indent-aware merges matter more than raw size
Math / scientific notation~0.30 tok/char + digit-splitLaTeX/Unicode operators as single tokens; single-digit split stabilizes arithmetic
Agentic syntax (JSON, tool schemas)near-code + reserved tokensReserved <tool_call>/<tool_result> tokens avoid spelling out structure

Tokenizer design choices

  • Byte-level BPE with 256-byte fallback — lossless on OCR/legacy/unseen text, never fully UNK.
  • Digit splitting (single digits) — better arithmetic.
  • Script-aware pre-tokenization + NFC — keep aksharas coherent, no cross-script merges.
  • Dedicated whitespace/tab and reserved agentic tokens.

256,000
Final trainable vocabulary — rounded up from the ~216k raw budget, matching Gemma 2/3[3][4] and leaving ~40k headroom for future language/domain expansion without a retrain. The embedding matrix is padded to 262,144 (2¹⁸) for hardware-friendly alignment — at d_model≈8192 that's ~2.1B tied embedding params (~5% of the 40B). Going past ~256k buys no fertility (IndicSuperTokenizer plateaus at ~200k[6]) and only inflates the embedding/softmax cost.

Takeaway: Llama/GPT are English-efficient but Indic-punishing; Sarvam is Indic-efficient but its 68k vocab starves code/English — a trade we can't accept given the coding/agentic objective. A large 256k vocab lets Bharat-40B stay English/code-competitive and dedicate the majority of vocab to Indian scripts + code, hitting near-Sarvam Indic fertility without giving up frontier coding.

9.Key trade-offs accepted

  • Indic web is oversampled ~5× its natural crawl frequency — accepting slight English-benchmark softness for the core India-first requirement.
  • Tier-2 languages get a fixed small vocab share and higher fertility rather than being dropped — coverage for 260M+ speakers beats leaderboard efficiency.
  • 256k vocab (vs a Sarvam-style 68–100k) because this model must carry heavy code/math/agentic token demand simultaneously with 22-language coverage; Sarvam's smaller vocab worked only because it targeted ~10 languages with lighter code ambitions[5].
  • ~10T over-trained tokens (vs ~1T Chinchilla-optimal) — extra pre-training compute traded for cheaper, stronger inference.
